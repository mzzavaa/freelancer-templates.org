  - title: "Announcement Formats"
    description: "Explore different announcement video formats that capture attention and communicate your product's value proposition effectively."
    columns: 3
    cards:
      - type: "rule"
        title: "Why Video Launches Work"
        body: "Video announcements outperform static posts because motion stops the scroll in crowded feeds, features can be shown in action rather than described, music and animation create emotional impact, and video content is 40x more likely to be shared."
        source: "Video Marketing Research"

      - type: "tip"
        title: "The Hook-Reveal-CTA Structure"
        body: "Structure your announcement in three parts: Hook (0-3s) grabs attention with a bold statement or visual, Reveal (3-20s) showcases the product or feature, and CTA (final 5s) tells viewers exactly what to do next."

      - type: "example"
        title: "SaaS Feature Announcement"
        body: "A project management tool announces new features using the Announcement template with a strategic launch sequence."
        list:

          - "Full feature reveal on launch day"
          - "Tutorial video the day after"
          - "Recap video one week later"

      - type: "rule"
        title: "Optimal Announcement Length"
        body: "Match video length to platform and purpose. Instagram Feed: 30-60 seconds. Instagram Reels: 15-30 seconds. TikTok: 15-60 seconds. YouTube: 60-120 seconds. LinkedIn: 30-60 seconds."
        source: "Platform Best Practices"

  - title: "Feature Reveals"
    description: "Master techniques for revealing new features in ways that highlight benefits and create memorable moments."
    columns: 2
    cards:
      - type: "rule"
        title: "Show, Don't Tell"
        body: "Demonstrate features in action rather than describing them. Screen recordings, product demos, and before/after comparisons are more compelling than text explanations. Viewers retain 95% of a message when watching video vs. 10% when reading."
        source: "Visual Learning Research"

      - type: "example"
        title: "App Update Announcement"
        body: "A mobile app company announces major updates using the Release Notes template with a clear structure."
        list:
          - "Hook: \"Big update alert!\" with app icon"
          - "Problem: What users asked for"
          - "Solution: New features in action"
          - "Benefits: Key improvements summarized"
          - "CTA: \"Update now\" with app store links"

      - type: "tip"
        title: "Feature List Animation"
        body: "Use staggered animations to reveal features one by one. Each feature enters sequentially with a slight delay (300ms works well), creating a sense of building value and keeping viewers engaged."

      - type: "example"
        title: "Feature Reveal Elements"
        body: "Visual elements that make feature reveals more impactful and memorable."
        table:
          headers:
            - "Element"
            - "Impact"
          rows:
            - ["Screen recording", "Shows real functionality"]
            - ["Icon + text combo", "Quick feature scanning"]
            - ["Before/after split", "Demonstrates improvement"]
            - ["Animated checkmarks", "Conveys completion/success"]

      - type: "rule"
        title: "Benefit-Focused Messaging"
        body: "Lead with benefits, not features. Instead of \"New dark mode added,\" say \"Easier on your eyes at night.\" Instead of \"50% faster performance,\" say \"Get more done in less time.\" Benefits resonate emotionally."
        source: "Marketing Psychology"

      - type: "tip"
        title: "Device Frame Context"
        body: "Show your app or product within a device frame (iPhone, laptop, etc.) to provide context and make the feature feel real. The Product Launch template includes device frame options for professional presentation."

  - title: "Countdown Builders"
    description: "Build anticipation and excitement with countdown videos that create urgency and keep your audience engaged before launch."
    columns: 3
    cards:
      - type: "rule"
        title: "Psychology of Countdowns"
        body: "Countdowns create anticipation through the Zeigarnik effect—people remember incomplete tasks better than completed ones. A countdown keeps your launch \"incomplete\" in viewers' minds, driving them to return for the reveal."
        source: "Behavioral Psychology"

      - type: "example"
        title: "E-commerce Product Drop"
        body: "A fashion brand creates hype for new collections using the Countdown Hype template with a strategic content calendar."
        table:
          headers:

          rows:
            - ["-7", "Teaser with silhouette"]
            - ["-3", "Sneak peek details"]
            - ["-1", "Final countdown"]
            - ["0", "Full launch reveal"]
            - ["+1", "Behind the scenes"]

      - type: "tip"
        title: "Teaser Content Strategy"
        body: "Reveal just enough to intrigue without giving everything away. Show silhouettes, blurred images, partial text, or cryptic hints. Each teaser should build on the last, creating a narrative arc toward the reveal."

      - type: "example"
        title: "Countdown Elements"
        body: "Key visual elements that make countdown videos effective at building anticipation."
        list:
          - "Large, animated countdown numbers"
          - "\"Coming Soon\" or \"Launching In\" text"
          - "Partial product reveals (silhouettes)"
          - "Date and time announcement"
          - "\"Set a reminder\" CTA"

      - type: "rule"
        title: "Countdown Timing"
        body: "Start your countdown 7-14 days before launch for major products, 3-5 days for features or updates. Post countdown updates at consistent times to train your audience when to expect news."
        source: "Launch Marketing Best Practices"

      - type: "tip"
        title: "Multi-Platform Countdowns"
        body: "Create countdown videos in multiple aspect ratios for different platforms. The Countdown Hype template supports 16:9 (YouTube), 9:16 (Reels/TikTok), and 1:1 (Feed) formats for maximum reach."

  - title: "Launch Day Content"
    description: "Execute a successful launch day with coordinated video content that maximizes visibility and drives immediate action."
    columns: 2
    cards:
      - type: "rule"
        title: "Launch Day Timing"
        body: "Post your main launch video when your audience is most active. For B2B: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am. For B2C: varies by platform, but evenings and weekends often perform well. Schedule follow-up posts throughout the day."
        source: "Social Media Analytics"

      - type: "example"
        title: "Course Launch Campaign"
        body: "An online educator launches new courses using the Course Promo template with a multi-week campaign."
        list:
          - "Week 1: Problem-focused awareness content"
          - "Week 2: Behind-the-scenes and sneak peeks"
          - "Week 3: Full trailer and early bird pricing"
          - "Week 4: Testimonials and cart open"

      - type: "tip"
        title: "Launch Video Checklist"
        body: "Before publishing, verify: hook captures attention in first 3 seconds, key benefit is clear within 10 seconds, product is shown in action, brand elements are present, text is readable on mobile, CTA is clear and actionable."

      - type: "example"
        title: "Launch Day Content Mix"

        table:

      - type: "rule"
        title: "Urgency Elements"
        body: "Create legitimate urgency with limited-time offers, early bird pricing, or limited availability. Display these prominently in your launch video. Avoid fake urgency—it damages trust and can violate advertising standards."
        source: "Ethical Marketing Guidelines"

      - type: "tip"
        title: "Real-Time Engagement"
        body: "Be active on launch day. Respond to comments quickly, share user reactions, and post updates throughout the day. Consider going live to answer questions and build excitement in real-time."

    description: "Extend your launch momentum with follow-up content that reinforces your message and converts interested viewers into customers."
    columns: 3

      - type: "rule"
        title: "The 7-Touch Rule"
        body: "Marketing research shows prospects need 7+ touchpoints before converting. Your launch video is just the beginning. Plan follow-up content that reinforces your message across multiple channels over days or weeks."
        source: "Marketing Research"

      - type: "example"

          - "Day +14: Case study or success story"

      - type: "tip"
        title: "User-Generated Content"
        body: "Encourage customers to share their experience with your product. Repost and feature user-generated content in follow-up videos. This provides social proof and extends your reach through their networks."
